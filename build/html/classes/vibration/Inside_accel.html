What's Inside An Accelerometer?

The following pictures are of the inside of an accelerometer donated to WSU by PCB.

It is a shear type accelerometer. The ring (dark "hoop") is a shape memory alloy. It is used to compress the masses (arcs) onto the piezoceramic material (dark bricks) against the triangular post. As the device is moved in the direction of the post, the inertia of the masses causes a shear in the piezoceramic material. This creates a charge proportional to the shear load, which is in turn proportional to the acceleration (F = m a). On top of the post sits the amplification circuitry that prevents the signal from degrading between the piezoceramic and the measurement device (data acquisition board...).
